GCS-Tacotron: End-to-End Code-Switched TTS with Graph Representation of Prosody Boundary

Abstract

    Code-switched text-to-speech (TTS) has attracted more and more attentions in recent years because of the increasing international exchanges. With the availability of a large number of monolingual corpora, code-switched speech synthesis is possible. However, one of the challenges of code-switched TTS is the naturalness and fluency of switching between different languages. In order to synthesize smooth code-switched speech, we propose a novel TTS model, called GCS-Tacotron. It is a variant of Tacotron for Mandarin-English mixed-lingual TTS that introduces prosodic information via graph neural network (GNN). In GCS-Tacotron, we first extend the prosodic graph for Mandarin text to Mandarin-English mixed-lingual text, then encode the prosodic graph with GNN, and integrate the graphical representation of code-switched prosodic information to the model. Furthermore, methods of Continual Learning are introduced to solve the Catastrophic Forgetting when using monolingual corpora to fine-tune the model which is pre-trained on monolingual corpora of Mandarin and English. Experiments show that the proposed model can synthesize more natural code-switched speech than the vanilla code-switched Tacotron.

Intex Term —— Text-To-Speech, Code-Switching, Graph Neural Network, Continual Learning
